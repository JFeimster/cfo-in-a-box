# Report Templates Overview

CFO-in-a-Box reports should be practical, founder-readable, and decision-oriented. Each report must separate known data from assumptions and end with actions.

## Universal report rules

Every report should include:

1. Scope and period reviewed
2. Inputs used
3. Known data
4. Assumptions
5. Key metrics
6. Findings
7. Risks
8. Opportunities
9. Recommended next actions
10. Missing data
11. Compliance note when relevant

## Financial Health Report

Use for broad analysis of P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, or mixed uploads.

Sections:

- Executive Summary
- Data Reviewed
- Key Metrics
- Revenue and Margin Review
- Cash Flow Review
- Debt and Working Capital Review
- Risks
- Opportunities
- Recommended Actions: 7 / 30 / 90 Days
- Assumptions and Missing Data

## Cash Flow Forecast Report

Use for 13-week or monthly forecasting.

Sections:

- Forecast Summary
- Forecast Period
- Assumptions
- Cash Flow Table
- Low-Cash Warnings
- Best/Base/Worst Cases
- Recommended Corrections
- Missing Data

## Funding Readiness Report

Use for readiness scoring and funding prep.

Sections:

- Funding Readiness Score
- Category Breakdown
- Score Interpretation
- Strengths
- Readiness Gaps
- Improvement Plan
- Funding Document Checklist
- Disclaimer

## Cost Leak Report

Use for expense cleanup and burn reduction.

Sections:

- Expense Leak Summary
- Top Opportunities
- Estimated Monthly Savings
- Risk Level by Cut
- Quick Wins
- Strategic Reviews
- Cuts to Avoid Without More Context
- 30-Day Cleanup Plan

## Weekly Financial Review

Use for operating cadence.

Sections:

- Cash Position
- Revenue Pulse
- Expense Check
- AR/AP Watchlist
- Debt and Payroll Watch
- Red/Yellow/Green Status
- Decisions Needed
- Owner Action Items

## Monthly Advisor Memo

Use for Founder OS and Advisor Desk workflows.

Sections:

- Memo Title
- Executive Summary
- Key Metrics
- What Changed
- Risks and Watch Items
- Recommended Actions
- Advisor Notes
- Assumptions and Limitations

## Style guidance

Write like a practical fractional CFO. Be direct, specific, and calm. Avoid vague advice such as "increase revenue" or "reduce expenses" unless paired with concrete actions.

Good:

> Contractor spend increased 22% while revenue only increased 6%. Review active contractor scopes this week and pause non-critical work until margin stabilizes.

Bad:

> Operational expenditures may require additional optimization.
